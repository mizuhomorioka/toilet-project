Where are you, Restrooms in Manhattan!

Mizuho Morioka

August 6th 2023

Last September, when I first arrived in New York, I was out at a supermarket in Chinatown. The outing turned out to be longer than I expected, and I realized it when I got on the train to go home. I needed to go to the bathroom. But where is the bathroom?

In Japan, most subway stations have toilets. In addition, you can find clean restrooms at most of the convenience stores in town.ã€€If you don't see a convenience store, you can go to Starbucks or other fastfood stores.

What about Manhattan, however? There are no restrooms on the subway. Worst of all, when I searched for Starbucks and headed there, I was refused, saying that they do not rent restrooms.

At that time I took 40 minutes to get home and was fine. But after that I still had trouble finding a bathroom in New York. Nothing is sadder, especially when you go to a place you think has a restroom and it doesn't. Just as in Starbucks, some stores rent restrooms and some do not.

So I tracked down places that have restrooms for guests and where I could borrow them quickly (No need to wait for the waiter to show you where to sit, or just pay a few bucks for something like coffee).

First, Let's look at Upper West Side, where I live. when I searched "restroom" on Google, it indicates only these blue dots. They seem to public restrooms in central park.

But I already know that Treder Joe's on 86th Street is allowing customers to use their restroom. It's this orange dot.

Also, this nice coffee shop "Daily Provisions" allows customers to use their restroom. The staffs are very nice.

The "Sweetgreen" salad chain sometimes lacks restrooms or asks for a PIN. But this store had a large eat-in area and a restroom to borrow.

Unlike Starbucks, most of "JOE & THE JUICE" shops allow customers to use their restrooms. This palce was also.

I kept on trucking and I found at least 19 stores with restrooms available in the Upper West between 59st to 96st.

Only half of Starbucks allow their toiles, and that was at 60st and 79st. Shake Shack and McDonald's seem to be renting. What about the rest of the time when you are having trouble finding a restroom in Central Park?

Take a look at midtown and downtown area.

From the survey, it appears that Treader Joe's and Whole Foods Market almost always have restrooms. As for the most likely locations of restrooms, the Treader Joe's and Whole Foods locations I looked up on Google are the green flower locations.

Additionally, I put some of the places I have checked that have restrooms. These orange dots are "Joe & JUICE" I checked their bathrooms.

Also, I found several other palces where they have restrooms. For example, McDonalds at Times Square has a clean bathrooms, and coffee shop "Pret A Manger" near Times Square also has a nice one.

Lastly, I introduce several my favorite restrooms near touristic areas. One is Whole Foods Market at Columbus Circle. It is located in the basement of Department Store, very accessible.

Next one is Macy's at 34st. This tradditional department store has very clean restrooms.

And Oculus near 9.11 museum. The building is new and beautiful, so are their restrooms.

Apparently, There are more restrooms than I thought in Manhattan. Still, you had better go to restrooms whenever you find them.
